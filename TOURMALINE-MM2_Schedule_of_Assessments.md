# TOURMALINE-MM2 Schedule of Assessments
## NCT01850524

### Trial Overview
- **Population:** Newly Diagnosed Multiple Myeloma (NDMM), Transplant-Ineligible
- **Phase:** Phase 3, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled
- **Sample Size:** N ≈ 706 patients

### Treatment Regimen (28-day cycles)
- **Ixazomib (or placebo):** 4.0 mg orally on Days 1, 8, 15
- **Lenalidomide:** 25 mg orally on Days 1-21 (or 10 mg if CrCl ≤60 mL/min)
- **Dexamethasone:** 40 mg orally on Days 1, 8, 15, 22

**Note:** Treatment continued until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity

## Special Assessment Notes

### Minimal Residual Disease (MRD) Assessment

**Population:** Patients achieving Complete Response (CR) or better

**Method:** 
- Bone marrow aspirate
- Flow cytometry with sensitivity of 10^-5 (1 myeloma cell per 100,000 normal cells)
- Performed at time of suspected CR and confirmed at subsequent assessment

**Significance:**
- MRD negativity associated with improved PFS and OS
- Important endpoint for NDMM population

### Pharmacokinetic (PK) Sampling

- **Population:** Subset of patients (sparse sampling strategy)
- **Timing:** Selected cycles (typically Cycle 1, Day 1)
- **Samples:** Pre-dose and post-dose time points per PK schedule
- **Analysis:** Population PK modeling to characterize drug exposure

### Biomarker Assessments

#### Blood Samples (Baseline)
- **Germline DNA:** Genetic variants in proteasome genes (PSMB1), NFKB pathway (TRAF-3)
- **Circulating Proteasome Levels:** Prognostic biomarker
- **RNA Analysis:** Gene expression profiling (if applicable)
- **Plasma/Serum Biomarkers:** Cytokines, inflammatory markers

#### Tumor Samples (Bone Marrow - Baseline)
- **Gene Expression Profiling:** High-risk vs standard-risk signatures
- **Somatic Mutations:** KRAS, NRAS, BRAF, TP53, ATM, etc.
- **Copy Number Alterations:** Gain 1q21, del 1p, hyperdiploidy
- **Cytogenetics Confirmation:** FISH for high-risk abnormalities
- **Relapse Samples:** If patient relapses, repeat bone marrow analysis

### Response Assessment Criteria (IMWG 2011)

Assessed every 4 weeks by Independent Review Committee (IRC):

**Positive Response Categories:**
- **Stringent CR (sCR):** CR + normal FLC ratio + <5% clonal plasma cells by immunohistochemistry
- **Complete Response (CR):** Negative immunofixation serum/urine, <5% plasma cells in bone marrow
- **Very Good Partial Response (VGPR):** Serum/urine M-protein detectable by immunofixation but not electrophoresis, or ≥90% reduction in M-protein + urine M-protein <100 mg/24h
- **Partial Response (PR):** ≥50% reduction in serum M-protein + reduction in 24h urinary M-protein by ≥90% or to <200 mg/24h

**Non-Response Categories:**
- **Minimal Response (MR):** 25-49% M-protein reduction (not used in primary analysis)
- **Stable Disease (SD):** Not meeting criteria for CR, VGPR, PR, MR, or PD
- **Progressive Disease (PD):** ≥25% increase in serum/urine M-protein (minimum 5 g/L or 200 mg/24h), bone marrow plasma cells (minimum 10%), new bone lesions, or hypercalcemia

### Dose Modification Guidelines

**Ixazomib:**
- Starting dose: 4.0 mg
- Dose level -1: 3.0 mg
- Dose level -2: 2.3 mg
- Discontinue if unable to tolerate 2.3 mg

**Lenalidomide:**
- Starting dose: 25 mg (or 10 mg if CrCl ≤60 mL/min)
- Dose reductions based on hematologic/non-hematologic toxicity
- Can be escalated to 15 mg, then 25 mg if renal function improves

**Dexamethasone:**
- Starting dose: 40 mg
- Reduce to 20 mg for patients >75 years or with comorbidities
- Further reductions as needed for tolerability

## Key Differences from TOURMALINE-MM1

### Patient Population
- **NDMM vs RRMM:** Transplant-ineligible newly diagnosed patients
- **No prior therapy:** Patients are treatment-naïve
- **Different prognostic factors:** Better baseline disease characteristics expected

### Special Assessments for NDMM
- **MRD Assessment:** More emphasis on achieving MRD negativity
- **Long-term Follow-up:** Potentially longer PFS and OS
- **Quality of Life:** Greater focus on maintaining function in older/ineligible patients

### Treatment Duration
- **Extended Treatment:** Many patients may receive therapy for prolonged periods (years)
- **Maintenance Considerations:** Some patients may continue until progression

## Data Elements Critical for AI/ML Modeling

### Baseline Characteristics (Time 0)
- **Demographics:** Age, sex, race, BSA, weight
- **Disease Features:** M-protein type, ISS/R-ISS stage, plasma cell %
- **Cytogenetics:** High-risk vs standard-risk
- **Organ Function:** Renal (CrCl), hepatic (LFTs), cardiac
- **Performance Status:** ECOG
- **Comorbidities:** Diabetes, cardiovascular disease, etc.

### Treatment Exposure (Longitudinal)
- **Ixazomib Dosing:** Actual dates and doses (Days 1, 8, 15)
- **Lenalidomide Dosing:** Actual dates and doses (Days 1-21)
- **Dexamethasone Dosing:** Actual dates and doses (Days 1, 8, 15, 22)
- **Dose Modifications:** Holds, reductions, delays with reasons and dates
- **Treatment Discontinuation:** Date and reason
- **Adherence/Compliance:** % of planned doses received

### Pharmacokinetic Data (if available)
- **Drug Concentrations:** Ixazomib plasma levels at specified timepoints
- **PK Parameters:** Cmax, AUC, clearance, volume of distribution (if calculated)

### Pharmacodynamic Biomarkers (Every 4 weeks)
- **M-protein (SPEP/UPEP):** Absolute values and % change from baseline
- **Serum Free Light Chains:** Kappa, lambda, ratio
- **Hematologic Parameters:** Hemoglobin, ANC, platelets
- **Bone Marrow Plasma Cells:** At baseline, CR confirmation, relapse

### Safety Data (Continuous)
- **Adverse Events:** Grade, onset date, resolution date, relationship to study drugs
- **Laboratory Abnormalities:** Dates and values
- **Dose-Limiting Toxicities:** Type and timing

### Clinical Outcomes
- **Response:** Best response achieved, time to response, duration of response
- **Progression-Free Survival:** Date of progression or death, censoring info
- **Overall Survival:** Date of death or last contact
- **Subsequent Therapies:** All post-progression treatments
- **MRD Status:** Results and dates of MRD assessments
